Israeli Occupation Prisons

Israeli occupation established many detention camps after the June 1967 war in which Israeli occupation occupied the West Bank, including Jerusalem and the Gaza Strip.

Acre Prison

Also known as Akko Prison is a former prison and current museum in Acre, occupied Palestine.

Ayalon Prison

Also known by its old name Ramla Prison is a maximum-security prison in Ramla, occupied Palestine.

Ashkelon Central Prison

It was established during the British Mandate era as the headquarters of the British Army's command in Ashkelon

Al-Maskobiya Prison

Al-Maskobiya Prison is located in the northern part of the city of Jerusalem, within the so-called “Russian Clock”

Nafkha Prison

This prison, which is 100 km from the city of Beersheba and 200 km from the city of Jerusalem, is considered one of the harshest Israeli occupation prisons.

Damon Prison

This prison was built since the time of the British Mandate as a warehouse for smoke
