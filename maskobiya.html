Al Maskobiya Prison

The Russian Compound in Jerusalem, it is one of the oldest areas in the center of Jerusalem. Its establishment dates back to the end of the Ottoman era in Palestine. It is located in the west of Jerusalem, near the border that separated it from the eastern part before the 1967 war

The complex was built in 1864 in the classical style by the Imperial Orthodox Palestinian Society, and with the support of the Tsar of Russia to serve Russian pilgrims to the Holy Land. Al-Maskoubiya extends over an area of ​​68,000 square meters between Jaffa Street and Al-Anbiya Street, hundreds of meters from the Jerusalem wall.

The complex includes a large Russian Orthodox church, the Holy Trinity Cathedral, a consulate, a hospital, a shopping center, a church and two large hostels for Russian pilgrims. Britain turned part of this complex into a police headquarters and detention center after its occupation of Jerusalem in 1917, and Israel has maintained this situation to this day since its takeover of the west of the city in 1948.
